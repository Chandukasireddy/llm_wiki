# Iceland

**Summary**: A guide to road-tripping and exploring the natural wonders of Iceland.

**Sources**: `europe_nordic_west.md`

**Last updated**: 2026-06-14

---

Iceland is characterized by dramatic volcanic landscapes, glaciers, and geothermal fields (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).

## The Golden Circle
The Golden Circle is a popular 300-kilometer loop starting from Reykjavik (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`):
* **Thingvellir National Park**: This is a geological area where tectonic plates pull apart. The **Silfra Fissure** offers diving/snorkeling in extremely clear glacial water (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).
* **Geysir**: Home to the **Strokkur** geyser, which erupts up to 20m high every 6–10 minutes (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).
* **Gullfoss**: A powerful double-tier waterfall plunging into a deep canyon (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).

## The South Coast
* **Seljalandsfoss**: A waterfall with a path allowing visitors to walk behind the water (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).
* **Skógafoss**: A 25m wide, 60m drop waterfall with a 527-step staircase to a viewing platform (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).
* **Reynisfjara**: A black sand beach near Vík with basalt columns (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`). Sneaker waves pose a fatal threat here; tourists are cautioned to stay far back from the water (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).
* **Sólheimajökull**: A glacier that hosts guided crampon walks (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).
* **Jökulsárlón & Diamond Beach**: Floating icebergs drift in a deep blue lagoon and wash ashore on the contrasting black sand (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).

## Logistics & Camping
* **F-Roads**: High-altitude gravel roads that require a 4WD vehicle and involve unbridged river crossings (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).
* **Campsites**: Wild camping is illegal. Travelers must stay in designated campervan campsites (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).
