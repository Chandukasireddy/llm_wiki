# Italy

**Summary**: A guide to exploring the ancient monuments, canals, and coastlines of Italy.

**Sources**: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`

**Last updated**: 2026-06-14

---

Italy is known for its historic cities, water-based transit systems, and regional food culture (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).

## Rome
* **Colosseum & Roman Forum**: Advanced tickets are required. The *Full Experience* ticket provides access to the arena floor and underground gladiator chambers (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).
* **Vatican City**: The Vatican Museums and Sistine Chapel can be toured with early access or during summer evening openings. The dress code strictly requires covering both shoulders and knees (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).
* **Trastevere**: A historic district across the Tiber River famous for traditional Roman pasta (Cacio e Pepe, Carbonara, Amatriciana). Recommended spots include *Da Enzo al 29* and *Frigidarium* for gelato (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).

## Venice
A floating city consisting of 118 islands (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).
* **Transit**: Travelers should purchase **Vaporetto** water bus passes instead of renting expensive private water taxis. Line 1 is the best slow sightseeing route along the Grand Canal (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).
* **St. Mark's Campanile**: The bell tower offers panoramic views of the Venetian lagoon (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).
* **Murano & Burano**: Outer islands accessible by vaporetto. Murano is known for glass blowing, while Burano is famous for lace-making, colored homes, and *Bussolai* cookies (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).

## Amalfi Coast
* **Positano**: Famous for cliffside pastel houses. Visitors are advised to prepare for walking up steep staircases (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).
* **Path of the Gods**: A scenic 10-km hiking trail between Bomerano and Nocelle. Hiking from east to west goes downhill and is recommended (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).
