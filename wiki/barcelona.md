# Barcelona

**Summary**: A detailed guide to Antoni Gaudí's modernist architecture, street festivals, and food culture in Barcelona.

**Sources**: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`

**Last updated**: 2026-06-14

---

Barcelona is a coastal city in Catalonia defined by its unique art movements and lively public events (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).

## Gaudí Architecture
Antoni Gaudí's modernist designs are prominent architectural features of the city (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`):
* **La Sagrada Família**: A basilica under construction since 1882. Its interior columns branch like trees, and its stained-glass windows project a spectrum of light. The towers can be climbed for city views (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).
* **Park Güell**: A colorful public park covered in ceramic mosaics, showing organic stone structures overlooking the Mediterranean (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).

## September Festivals & Weather
* **Weather**: In September, August's heavy tourist crowds depart, the sea remains warm, and the climate is comfortable (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).
* **La Mercè Festival**: Barcelona's largest street party, taking place in late September. Events include **castellers** (human towers), **correfocs** (fire runs with sparklers), and outdoor music stages (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).

## Gastronomy & Tapas
* **Born & Gràcia**: These historic neighborhoods are recommended for local tapas, escaping the tourist crowds of Las Ramblas (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`). Try *Patatas Bravas* (fried potato pieces with spicy sauce) or blistered *Pimientos de Padrón* (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).
* **Sunday Vermut**: A popular weekend tradition of drinking sweet vermouth with olives and canned seafood (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).
