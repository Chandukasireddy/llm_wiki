# Norway

**Summary**: A guide to the vertical mountains, deep fjords, and coastal islands of Norway.

**Sources**: `europe_nordic_west.md`

**Last updated**: 2026-06-14

---

Norway is famous for its massive mountains, deep blue fjords, and scenic railways (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).

## The Lofoten Islands
Located in Northern Norway above the Arctic Circle, this archipelago features dramatic peaks and red fisherman's cabins (*rorbuer*) (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`):
* **Reine**: A beautiful village framed by the Reinebringen peak (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).
* **Reinebringen Hike**: A steep climb of 1,560 Sherpa-built stone steps offering a 360-degree view of Reine and the surrounding ocean (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).
* **Henningsvær**: A fishing village home to the world-famous Henningsvær Stadium, a football pitch built on a rocky islet (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).
* **Unstad Beach**: A cold-water surfing spot popular for riding waves under the midnight sun or Northern Lights (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).

## Fjords & Scenic Trains
* **Geirangerfjord**: A UNESCO site with steep cliffs and waterfalls like the *Seven Sisters* and *The Suitor* (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).
* **Nærøyfjord**: A narrow Sognefjord branch (only 250m wide at some points) bordered by 1,800m high cliffs (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).
* **The Bergen Line**: A scenic railway between Oslo and Bergen crossing the Hardangervidda plateau (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).
* **Flåm Railway**: Connects with the Bergen Line at Myrdal and plunges down a steep valley to Flåm (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).
