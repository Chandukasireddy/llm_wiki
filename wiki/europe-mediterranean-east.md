# Southern & Central Europe Guide

**Summary**: A travel guide summary covering Mediterranean coastlines, ancient ruins, regional food culture, and imperial landmarks in Italy, Spain, Greece, Austria, and Hungary.

**Sources**: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`

**Last updated**: 2026-06-14

---

This source document is authored by [[mateo-silva]] (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`). It covers five major regions:

### 1. [[italy]]
Contains comprehensive information on navigating Rome's ancient sites, dress codes for Vatican City, finding authentic pasta in Trastevere, water transit (Vaporetto) passes in Venice, and hiking the Path of the Gods along the Amalfi Coast (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).

### 2. [[spain]]
Showcases Andalusian and Catalan traditions (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`). Highlights include Gaudí's modernist structures and the late-September La Mercè street party in [[barcelona]], alongside Seville's Real Alcázar and authentic Flamenco clubs (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).

### 3. [[greece]]
Explores historical Athens (Acropolis, Plaka, and Anafiotika) and Aegean islands, specifically Santorini caldera trails and Milos's unique white-rock Sarakiniko beach (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).

### 4. [[austria]]
Features Vienna's imperial palaces (Schönbrunn) and slow-living Coffee House customs (Melange coffee, Sachertorte) (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).

### 5. [[hungary]]
Focuses on Budapest's geothermal spas (Széchenyi, Gellért), Jewish Quarter ruin bars (Szimpla Kert), and river cruises passing the lit Parliament building (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).
