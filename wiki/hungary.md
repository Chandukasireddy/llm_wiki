# Hungary

**Summary**: A guide to the hot springs, thermal baths, and ruin bars of Budapest, Hungary.

**Sources**: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`

**Last updated**: 2026-06-14

---

Hungary is a Central European country distinguished by its geothermal water network and modern cultural venues (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).

## Budapest
Budapest is the capital city, situated along the Danube River (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).
* **Thermal Baths**: 
  * **Széchenyi**: Europe's largest medicinal bath, featuring yellow neo-baroque outdoor pools where locals play chess in the steam (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).
  * **Gellért**: Famous for Art Nouveau indoor pools, mosaic columns, and stained-glass windows (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).
* **Ruin Bars**: Mismatched bars set up in abandoned pre-war structures in the old Jewish Quarter (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`). **Szimpla Kert** is the original ruin bar, featuring eclectic furniture and a Trabant car dining table (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).
* **Hungarian Parliament**: A Gothic Revival masterpiece on the edge of the Danube. It is best viewed lit up at night from a river cruise (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).
