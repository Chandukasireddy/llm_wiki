# United Kingdom

**Summary**: A guide to the food and museum scene of London and the rugged hikes in the Scottish Highlands.

**Sources**: `europe_nordic_west.md`

**Last updated**: 2026-06-14

---

The United Kingdom presents a contrast between London's urban food and cultural hubs and Scotland's rugged nature (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).

## London
* **Borough Market**: One of London's oldest markets. Famous for hot roast pork rolls (*Hobbs Roast*), fresh oysters, and British Stilton and Cheddar cheeses (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).
* **Museum Row**: Located in South Kensington, containing the **Natural History Museum**, **Science Museum**, and the **Victoria and Albert (V&A) Museum**. Admission is free, but timed-entry tickets must be booked online (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).
* **West End Theatre**: Deals can be found on the day of the show at the TKTS booth in Leicester Square or using the TodayTix app (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).

## The Scottish Highlands
* **Glencoe**: A volcanic valley home to the Three Sisters mountains, the *Lost Valley* hiking trail, and the historic site of the 1692 Glencoe Massacre (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).
* **Isle of Skye**: Located in northern Scotland and connected by a bridge (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`):
  * **The Old Man of Storr**: A high rock pinnacle on the Trotternish Peninsula with a 45-minute hike (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).
  * **Fairy Pools**: Clear, cold glacial pools in the River Brittle that allow for wild swimming (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).
  * **Neist Point Lighthouse**: Located on Skye's westernmost point atop dramatic basalt cliffs (source: `europe_nordic_west.md`).
