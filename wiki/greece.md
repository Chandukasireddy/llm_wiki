# Greece

**Summary**: A travel guide to historical Athens and the volcanic landscapes of the Aegean islands.

**Sources**: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`

**Last updated**: 2026-06-14

---

Greece offers a mixture of classical history on the mainland and unique geological beaches in the Cyclades islands (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).

## Athens
* **The Acropolis**: The ancient fortress containing the **Parthenon**, **Erechtheion**, and **Temple of Athena Nike**. Gates open at 8:00 AM (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`). Sighted items are supported by the nearby **Acropolis Museum** (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).
* **Plaka & Anafiotika**: Plaka is Athens' historic heart. Bordering the Acropolis walls is **Anafiotika**, a scenic neighborhood built by builders from the island of Anafi featuring whitewashed stone walls, blue doors, and narrow corridors (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).

## The Aegean Islands
* **Santorini**:
  * **Caldera Hike**: A 10-km trail along the edge of the volcanic crater between Fira and Oia, taking 3–4 hours (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).
  * **Oia Sunset**: A popular viewing spot is the Byzantine Castle in Oia. Catamaran cruises and the Akrotiri Lighthouse are alternatives (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).
* **Milos**: A quieter island location (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).
  * **Sarakiniko Beach**: Renowned for white volcanic rock formations sculpted by waves, giving a lunar appearance (source: `europe_mediterranean_east.md`).
